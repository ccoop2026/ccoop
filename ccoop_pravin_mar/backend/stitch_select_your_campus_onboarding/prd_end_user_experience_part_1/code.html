PRD Part 1: End-User Experience

Defining the student and alumni journey for the ccoop.in marketplace. This document outlines the core interactions, financial commitment logic, and fulfillment cycles for the MVP.

groups

1. User Profile & Objectives

The Persona

The primary user is a current university student (ages 18-24) or young alumni (ages 22-30) who takes pride in their academic identity but feels disconnected from standard campus retail.

  • check_circle High demand for "exclusive" department-specific apparel.
  • check_circle Motivated by group-buying savings and community participation.

Key Pain Points

01.

Excessive Pricing

Retail merch is often overpriced due to middle-man markups.

02.

Poor Customization

Generic designs that don't reflect specific departments or batch pride.

3. The Two-Stage Pre-Order Flow

Stage 1: The Pledge (Soft Launch)

Users "Pledge" to buy without paying upfront. This serves as a demand discovery phase.

Zero-Payment Pledge

Stage 2: Payment & Commitment

Once MOQ is met, the campaign enters the 'Locked' phase. Users must confirm within 48-72 hours.

payments 50% Token Payment required to confirm order.
swap_horiz Variant Substitutions: If a specific color (e.g. Navy) fails MOQ but the product (Hoodie) succeeds, users are offered the winning variant.

5. Wallet & Financial Trust

Building Trust in the Crowd

Since campaigns depend on collective action, the wallet is the safety net for the student's money.

  • add

    The 110% Bonus: If a campaign fails after token payment, the student gets 100% refund + 10% credit bonus for the next drop.

  • account_balance

    Instant Withdrawal: Credits can be withdrawn to original payment method (UPI/Card) at any time without fees.
